About Me

Welcome to my world!


I am a second-year student in research-based master’s program in Robotics at Carnegie Mellon University, School of Computer Science, focusing on computer vision and machine learning. My research focuses on understanding human behavior through computer vision, machine learning and psychology. I have been working on building multi-modal (using video and audio) emotion recognition system and on understanding the influence of body posture, facial expressions and head motions on the behavior of people in interpersonal conversations.


Prior to joining CMU, I worked as a research staff at School Of Computer Science & Engineering, Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. Where I was working in the area of embedded computer vision, on implementing real time, hardware efficient object and pedestrian detection systems on embedded devices. Prior to that during my undergrad I did a double major in Physics and Electrical Engineering from BITS Pilani University, India.
